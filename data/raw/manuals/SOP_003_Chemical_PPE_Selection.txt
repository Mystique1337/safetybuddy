STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document ID: SOP-PPE-003
Title: Chemical-Specific PPE Selection Guide
Revision: 1.5 | Effective Date: 2024-06-01
Department: Environmental Health & Safety

1. PURPOSE
Provide guidance on selecting appropriate PPE for chemical handling operations based on Safety Data Sheet (SDS) requirements and OSHA standards.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all personnel who handle, transfer, mix, or may be exposed to hazardous chemicals.

3. PROCEDURE

Step 1: Obtain the SDS. Check Section 2 (Hazard Identification) and Section 8 (Exposure Controls/PPE).
Step 2: Identify exposure routes: skin contact, eye contact, inhalation, ingestion.
Step 3: Select PPE using the matrix below.
Step 4: Verify glove compatibility with manufacturer chemical resistance chart.
Step 5: Document on Form PPE-CHEM-01.

4. CHEMICAL-PPE MATRIX

Dilute acids (under 10 percent concentration): Nitrile gloves (8 mil minimum), chemical splash goggles, lab coat or chemical apron. Respiratory not required unless vapor above PEL. Emergency eyewash within 10 seconds travel per 1910.151(c).

Concentrated acids (over 10 percent): Butyl rubber or neoprene gloves (14 mil minimum), splash goggles AND face shield, half-face APR with acid gas cartridge if above PEL, chemical resistant apron and arm sleeves.

Organic solvents (acetone, toluene, MEK, xylene): Short exposure under 15 min use nitrile gloves. Extended exposure over 15 min use PVA or Silver Shield laminate gloves. Splash goggles required. OV cartridge respirator if above PEL.

Caustics and bases (sodium hydroxide, potassium hydroxide): Neoprene or butyl rubber gloves. Splash goggles AND face shield. Chemical resistant apron.

Flammable liquids: Nitrile gloves (non-sparking). Safety glasses minimum, splash goggles for pouring. FR clothing required, NO synthetic fabrics. Eliminate ignition sources, ground and bond containers.

Dusts and particulates: Nuisance dust below PEL use N95 facepiece. Toxic dust at or above PEL use P100 or HEPA cartridge APR, tight goggles, Tyvek coveralls and boot covers.

5. EMERGENCY RESPONSE

Eye contact: Flush at eyewash station minimum 15 minutes. Remove contacts if easily removable. Seek medical attention with SDS.

Skin contact: Remove contaminated clothing. Flush skin minimum 15 minutes. Do NOT use solvents to clean skin.

Inhalation: Move to fresh air. If breathing difficulty call emergency services. If cartridge breakthrough exit area and replace cartridge.

6. REFERENCES
OSHA 29 CFR 1910.132-138, 1910.1200, 1910.134, 1910.151(c), NIOSH Pocket Guide.
